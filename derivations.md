# Derivations for ATMO-612 HW 4

We consider a plane-parallel cloud layer with optical thickness

$$
\tau_c = 20,
$$

single-scattering albedo

$$
\tilde{\omega} = 0.95,
$$

and Henyey--Greenstein phase function

$$
P(\theta)=\frac{1-g^2}{\left(1+g^2-2g\cos\theta\right)^{3/2}},
\qquad g=0.8.
$$

The solar zenith and azimuth angles are

$$
\theta_0 = 35^\circ,
\qquad
\phi_0 = 0^\circ.
$$

We define

$$
\mu=\cos\theta,
\qquad
\mu_0=\cos\theta_0=\cos35^\circ=0.819152.
$$

The goal is to compute the di-directional reflection and transmission functions

$$
R(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
\qquad \text{and} \qquad
T(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
$$

using the successive-orders-of-scattering (SOS) method for

$$
\phi=30^\circ,\quad 90^\circ,\quad 120^\circ.
$$

## Diffuse radiance expansion

The total diffuse radiance is expanded in scattering order as

$$
I(\tau,\mu,\phi)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} I_n(\tau,\mu,\phi),
$$

where $I_n$ is the contribution from the $n$-th scattering order.

The direct solar beam is

$$
I_0(\tau,\mu,\phi)
=
F_0 e^{-\tau/\mu_0}\delta(\mu-\mu_0)\delta(\phi-\phi_0).
$$

The di-directional reflection and transmission functions are defined by

$$
R(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
=
\frac{\pi I^\uparrow(0,\mu,\phi)}{\mu_0F_0},
$$

$$
T(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
=
\frac{\pi I^\downarrow(\tau_c,\mu,\phi)}{\mu_0F_0}.
$$

Thus,

$$
R(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}R_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0),
$$

$$
T(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}T_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0),
$$

with

$$
R_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
=
\frac{\pi I_n^\uparrow(0,\mu,\phi)}{\mu_0F_0},
\qquad
T_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
=
\frac{\pi I_n^\downarrow(\tau_c,\mu,\phi)}{\mu_0F_0}.
$$

## Phase function

The Henyey--Greenstein phase function is

$$
P(\theta)=\frac{1-g^2}{\left(1+g^2-2g\cos\theta\right)^{3/2}}.
$$

For $g=0.8$,

$$
P(\theta)=\frac{0.36}{\left(1.64-1.6\cos\theta\right)^{3/2}}.
$$

Here $\theta$ is the scattering angle, not the propagation zenith angle.

## Reflection geometry

For reflection, the solar beam is downward while the scattered direction is upward. The scattering angle $\theta_R$ is determined from the dot product of the outgoing and incoming unit vectors:

$$
\cos\theta_R
=
-\mu\mu_0
+
\sqrt{1-\mu^2}\sqrt{1-\mu_0^2}\cos(\phi-\phi_0).
$$

Since $\phi_0=0^\circ$,

$$
\boxed{
\cos\theta_R
=
-\mu\mu_0
+
\sqrt{1-\mu^2}\sqrt{1-\mu_0^2}\cos\phi
}
$$

## Transmission geometry

For transmission, both the incident and outgoing directions are downward, so

$$
\cos\theta_T
=
\mu\mu_0
+
\sqrt{1-\mu^2}\sqrt{1-\mu_0^2}\cos(\phi-\phi_0).
$$

Since $\phi_0=0^\circ$,

$$
\boxed{
\cos\theta_T
=
\mu\mu_0
+
\sqrt{1-\mu^2}\sqrt{1-\mu_0^2}\cos\phi
}
$$

Because

$$
\mu_0=\cos35^\circ=0.819152,
\qquad
\sqrt{1-\mu_0^2}=\sin35^\circ=0.573576,
$$

the above become

$$
\cos\theta_R
=
-0.819152\,\mu+0.573576\sqrt{1-\mu^2}\cos\phi,
$$

$$
\cos\theta_T
=
\phantom{-}0.819152\,\mu+0.573576\sqrt{1-\mu^2}\cos\phi.
$$

# First-order scattering solution

## First-order source function

The first-order source function is generated by scattering of the direct beam:

$$
J_1(\tau,\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{\tilde{\omega}}{4\pi}
P(\theta)\,
F_0 e^{-\tau/\mu_0}.
$$

## Reflection

The first-order upward radiance at the top of the atmosphere is

$$
I_1^\uparrow(0,\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{\tilde{\omega}F_0}{4\pi}
P(\theta_R)
\frac{\mu_0}{\mu+\mu_0}
\left[
1-\exp\left(-\tau_c\left(\frac{1}{\mu}+\frac{1}{\mu_0}\right)\right)
\right].
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{
R_1(\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{\tilde{\omega}}{4}
\frac{P(\theta_R)}{\mu+\mu_0}
\left[
1-\exp\left(-\tau_c\left(\frac{1}{\mu}+\frac{1}{\mu_0}\right)\right)
\right]
}
$$

## Transmission

The first-order downward radiance at the bottom of the atmosphere is

$$
I_1^\downarrow(\tau_c,\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{\tilde{\omega}F_0}{4\pi}
P(\theta_T)
\frac{\mu_0}{\mu_0-\mu}
\left[
e^{-\tau_c/\mu_0}-e^{-\tau_c/\mu}
\right].
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{
T_1(\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{\tilde{\omega}}{4}
\frac{P(\theta_T)}{\mu_0-\mu}
\left[
e^{-\tau_c/\mu_0}-e^{-\tau_c/\mu}
\right]
}
\qquad (\mu\neq\mu_0)
$$

For the special case $\mu=\mu_0$, the limiting form is

$$
\boxed{
T_1(\mu_0,\phi)
=
\frac{\tilde{\omega}}{4}
P(\theta_T)\,
\frac{\tau_c}{\mu_0}
e^{-\tau_c/\mu_0}
}
$$

# Specific solar-viewing configurations

## $\phi=30^\circ$

Since

$$
\cos30^\circ=0.866025,
$$

we obtain

$$
\cos\theta_R^{(30)}
=
-0.819152\,\mu+0.496732\sqrt{1-\mu^2},
$$

$$
\cos\theta_T^{(30)}
=
\phantom{-}0.819152\,\mu+0.496732\sqrt{1-\mu^2}.
$$

## $\phi=90^\circ$

Since

$$
\cos90^\circ=0,
$$

we obtain

$$
\cos\theta_R^{(90)}=-0.819152\,\mu,
\qquad
\cos\theta_T^{(90)}=0.819152\,\mu.
$$

## $\phi=120^\circ$

Since

$$
\cos120^\circ=-\frac{1}{2},
$$

we obtain

$$
\cos\theta_R^{(120)}
=
-0.819152\,\mu-0.286788\sqrt{1-\mu^2},
$$

$$
\cos\theta_T^{(120)}
=
\phantom{-}0.819152\,\mu-0.286788\sqrt{1-\mu^2}.
$$

These expressions are inserted into

$$
P(\theta)=\frac{0.36}{\left(1.64-1.6\cos\theta\right)^{3/2}}
$$

to compute $R_1$ and $T_1$.

# Higher-order scattering

For $n\ge 2$, the source function is generated by the $(n-1)$-th order diffuse radiance:

$$
J_n(\tau,\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{\tilde{\omega}}{4\pi}
\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1
P(\theta)\,
I_{n-1}(\tau,\mu',\phi')\,d\mu' d\phi'.
$$

The corresponding formal solutions are

$$
I_n^\uparrow(0,\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{1}{\mu}\int_0^{\tau_c}
J_n(t,\mu,\phi)e^{-t/\mu}\,dt,
$$

$$
I_n^\downarrow(\tau_c,\mu,\phi)
=
\frac{1}{\mu}\int_0^{\tau_c}
J_n(t,\mu,\phi)e^{-(\tau_c-t)/\mu}\,dt.
$$

Hence the total SOS solution is

$$
\boxed{
R(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
=
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}R_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
}
$$

and

$$
\boxed{
T(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
=
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}T_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)
}
$$

In practical computation, the series is truncated after a finite number of orders:

$$
R(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)\approx \sum_{n=1}^{N}R_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0),
$$

$$
T(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0)\approx \sum_{n=1}^{N}T_n(\mu,\phi,\mu_0,\phi_0).
$$

# Conclusion

The successive-orders-of-scattering method expresses the reflection and transmission of a cloud layer as sums over individual scattering orders. The first-order reflection and transmission functions are obtained analytically, while higher-order terms are computed recursively from the source generated by the previous order. For the present cloud layer with large optical thickness $\tau_c=20$ and high single-scattering albedo $\tilde{\omega}=0.95$, multiple scattering is important and must be included to obtain the full solution.
